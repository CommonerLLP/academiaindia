This tracker is a research-adjacent tool built as data infrastructure first, user-interface second. The underlying corpus of advertisements is archived daily and is the durable public good; this dashboard is a replaceable thin layer on top.

Why you should not trust this tool as your primary source: India's HEI recruitment pages are heterogeneous, often PDF-only, sometimes JavaScript-rendered, and routinely restructured. Parsers break. When a parser breaks, an institution silently disappears from the feed. Check the Coverage tab to see which institutions were parsed in the last run.

What this tracker does not cover: State public universities; state-funded private universities; deemed-to-be universities; most research institutions (TIFR, CSIR labs, ICAR institutes); Samarth-only institutions (Samarth eGov's ToS forbids automated access). If an institution has migrated recruitment to Samarth exclusively, it is flagged and excluded.

On the politics of this object. Faculty and non-teaching advertisements in India are governed by a category-reservation regime — UR, SC, ST, OBC (NCL), EWS, PwBD — and many reserved-category posts are repeatedly re-advertised after Not Found Suitable declarations, then eventually de-reserved. A listings-only tracker surfaces the advertisement moment but cannot tell you what happened to the post afterwards. A future version will match re-advertisements of the same post code over time.
